Title: Freekeh Soup

Yield: 5

Cooking time: 1 hour

Source: Zaitoun

Ingredients

  • 1/2 tsp ground allspice
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 onions
  • 500 ml vegetable stock
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 can pinto beans
  • olive oil
  • 150 g freekeh
  • 2 Tbs lemon juice
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 sticks celery
  • yogurt (optional)
  • 15 g chives (optional)

Instructions

Heat the olive oil in a saucepan, add the onion and fry over a medium heat for 10-15 minutes, until soft.

Toast the coriander and cumin seeds by stirring them in a dry pan over a low heat for a minute or so until their aromas are released.
Crush them in a pestle and mortar and add to the onion with the allspice and garlic. Fry for another 2 minutes until fragrant.

Add the pinto beans, freekeh, carrots, celery, stock and 500 boiling water, cover and simmer for 45 minutes until the freekeh is quite soft.

Then season with half teaspoon pepper and 2 tablespoons each of olive oil and lemon juice. Depending on how salty your stock was, you may want to add a little more salt. Stir well and cook for 2 minutes.

To serve, ladle into warmed bowls, add generous dollops of natural yogurt, a sprinkling of chives and a drizzle of olive oil.
